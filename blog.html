Happee

Project

Happee was my first UX/UI project as a freelancer. I was hired to design an app for finding clean bathrooms nearby. First I had to research and validate the idea and then I designed an MVP for the product.

User Research

In the research process I looked for behavioral patterns depending on genre. However, most of the pain points were even, independly of the genre. Some of these pain points are: dirty bathroom, no toilet paper, and waiting in line (specially for women) Also, in the interviews, people with children complained about the trouble that represents taking a kid to a public restroom. Male parents complained about the absence of baby changers in male restrroms, while female parents complained about the trouble of taking a child to the bathroom in a small cubicle (it is hard to find famity bathrooms). Also paying in a restaurant for a small consumption, in order to use the bathroom, is usual among users. it is a major pain point, not because of the money, but because they pay, and then they find the bathroom dirty.

User Journey
Low fidelity Design

Laura required to find a bathroom as soon as posible, specially when she's taking care of the children. That is the reason why the ux strategy it is to show all the information she needs in a first sight. The idea of the product is to provide all the information (how clean it is, if there's any line, or if there's toilet paper) very quick to the user. So this information shoud be in real-time. The idea is to create an interface similary to the ones for tracking the traffic (think of Waze). It is important that users share info about public restrooms in real-time. Also as a business strategy, the idea is to offer visibility to restaurants, coffee shops and bars through their bathrooms. That way, since people is willing to make a consumption, the business will benefit directly

Branding

Brand attributes: Free, clean, happy, easy.

UI Design

I inspired in Laura who wants to illustrate children's books in the future, also in honour to all those parents who keep having trouble when they have to take their children to a public restroom. The UI required to be very visual so I designed a library of illustrated icons. The interface is white in order to convey feeling of cleanliness

UI Kit

I prepared a UI Kit for the developers. That way they could find all the components in Zepplin. Working with components helped me to achive an homogenic look through the interface. Also it was a small bite for starting to think in atomic design through components, something that I applied exhaustively in future projects of my carreer
